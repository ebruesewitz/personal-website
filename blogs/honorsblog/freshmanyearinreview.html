Freshman Year In Review

Well, my first year of college is officially over. As I’m sitting in my empty dorm room waiting for my RA to pick up my keys, I can’t help but look back at my first year at UC and reflect on my experiences here. This year has brought growth in two major areas of my life. I’ll try my best to highlight them below.


Personal Growth:

This year was the first time I was truly independent and it terrified me. I was responsible for my own schedule, budget, eating and sleeping patterns. I wish I could say that I did an excellent job of managing these areas of my life, but the truth is I struggled with almost every one of them. It was hard learning how to budget my time between classes, jobs, friendships and other responsibilities. I quickly learned that watching Netflix for 4 hours a day was not going to be an acceptable use of my time. I learned that I had to go to bed at a reasonable time if I had any hope of making my 8 am’s the next morning, and that in order to get to bed at a decent time, I needed to budget my free time effectively. I’m the kind of person who loves to fill every spare minute of my day with some form of activity. However, this year taught me that sometimes the best thing I can do for myself and for others is to simply be available. I learned that others feel valued when I’m available to help them with a project that comes up, and I feel much less stressed when I allow time in my day for the unexpected.

Living with a roommate also promoted growth in my communication skills—I learned that if you want someone to change a certain behavior, you have to graciously point it out to them. I learned how to talk through issues with a person rather than complain and gossip about them. And most importantly, I gained a new best friend.

I also gained a deep love for the city of Cincinnati this school year. My friend Kaitlyn and I would go on “Adventure Thursdays” every week to explore new places around the city. We went to several coffee shops, restaurants, and museums. A few of my favorite places included the Macaron Bar in OTR, Mount Adams Lookout, Streetpops, Aglamesis Bros Ice Cream, the Contemporary Arts Center, and Union Terminal. I realized that if you’re willing to step outside of your comfort zone and try something new, you’ll appreciate your city and its history a lot more.


Professional Growth:

As an engineering student, I am required to participate in a co-op program starting my second year at the university. Because of this, I learned a lot about professionalism through my Intro to Co-op course my second semester. I learned how to build a resumé that showcases my strengths as a professional. I learned that while I don’t have major-specific experience, I still have a lot of skills to offer a company; such as leadership and teamwork skills that I have built through leading Young Life and participating in other honors experiences. Once I gained confidence in myself as a professional, I quickly developed the communication skills necessary for interviews and career fairs. The career fair provided me with valuable information about different companies I could be co-oping for and gave me a better idea of what a chemical engineer might do after graduation. I also learned the importance of networking and was able to land a co-op at L’Oreal this fall because of the people I met at the career fair. I'm excited to start that co-op and learn more about applications of my degree.


In conclusion, I think the biggest lesson I’ll take away from this year is this:

Don’t be afraid to step outside of your comfort zone. It will cause you to grow in ways you didn’t think were possible and will teach you so much about yourself and the world around you. Sure, you might fail at first, but look at failures as a chance to learn and grow rather than a sign to quit.


Thanks for a great first year, UC. See you again in August.
