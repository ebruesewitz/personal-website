To The Dreamer

I am a big supporter of dreams.

I believe each of us is created with unique dreams and passions, and that’s what makes our culture work. We each have different aspirations for the future. Different ideas of success. Different approaches to life.

Yet, somewhere along the way, we lose sight of these dreams.

We let others take hold of our dreams and mold them to fit their own ideas of success. It happens so often, so gradually, that we don’t even realize it.

We don’t realize that the people who care about us are the ones doing it to us. We don’t realize that we’re doing it to the people we care about too.

We let people tell us our dreams will never amount to anything. And we start to believe them.

So we dismiss these dreams, writing them off as childish fantasies that will never grow into anything. And we find ourselves working toward a stable career at a Fortune 500 company because that’s what makes sense. It makes sense. And it’s comfortable. Yet, it feels empty. Incomplete. There has to be something more than this, right? More than the next big project. More than another promotion. More than the next paycheck we’ve worked so hard to earn.

See, that’s the problem with ignoring our dreams—they still come back to haunt us. We may not remember exactly what they were, but we know they existed. And deep down, we still long to make them a reality.

What are your dreams?

The ones you’ve lost sight of—the ones that seemed so crazy they could never be more than a fantasy.

Have you ever stopped to consider that maybe they could be reality?

What if we used our skills and worked together to make each other’s dreams reality? If we surrounded ourselves with people who nurtured our dreams and dreamt with us then maybe, just maybe, we could make something real out of them.

After all, that’s how true innovation begins: with a dream.

Companies like Google, Apple, Windows and Facebook all started with a dream. And that dream, when put into the right hands, is a very powerful thing. It can lead to change. To new ideas. To growth.

I encourage you to seriously consider your dreams: What would life look like if you fulfilled them? What is holding you back from achieving them? What steps can you take to reach them?

Maybe along the way youll find that the reality of your dream isn’t as spectacular as you thought it would be. That’s okay. Some dreams are meant to stay that way.

Just never stop dreaming.
